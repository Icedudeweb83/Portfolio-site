The first step in creating a slick, modern album cover for a classic film is finding a suitably large enough image with which to work. This one has a little wear, but is generally pretty clean. It's slightly rough and washed out, but has a lot of promise!

After evaluating the color scheme, the next step was determining how best to give the overall look a modest update; one that would help bring it back to life without investing too much time, but that would also honor the original intent and integrity of the image. The best choice, I decided, was to create a new blue gradient background image on which to "paint."

I then traced all the key graphics (the humans and the mountains) in Photoshop and carefully cleaned/color corrected them. The big question in my mind though was, "just how good can the logo look? Especially when I add new text such as credits and the tagline to the final cover?" I didn't think a faded, rastered version of the logo would really do the work justice in the end, so I decided to recreate it.

Now that I had all the essential artifacts looking good, I recreated the tagline and the Technicolor badge, and created custom credits to assemble the finished piece. Click the image to enlarge it!
