The official album cover from Intrada Records features the famous Drew Struzan image of Doc and Marty stepping out of the Delorean. However, as this is one of my favorite film series, I wanted to improve upon a few things. Naturally.

For one, the image is relatively low resolution (600x600 px). For another, the color scheme on both the logo and Alan Silvestri's composer credit is a little too muted. And finally, I wanted to have elements for alternate covers because... let's face it, this is one of the all-time great logos and using the logo style on the composer credit is a great idea!

What I discovered is that there are a variety of high quality BTTF images that really pop, but the available logos don't really do them justice. They're either too muted, have a color balance that's a little "off," or they're too small. So, to make life a little more fun, I decided to recreate both the logo and the composer credit. 😁

Fortunately the "Back to the Future" portion was super easy as a vectored version (with layers!) already existed. I separated the layers to adjust the gradient and stroke colors to what I had in mind, and then for the rest of the logo I traced scanned materials in Illustrator to create the remaining artifacts and layers. Happily the result is a bolder version with greater depth and definition.

For the composer credit I turned to MyFonts.com for the proper typeface. I typed it out in Illustrator and matched the gradient and stroke colors from the logo.

Working with the Perspective Grid Tool in Illustrator, and carefully checking my logo for reference, I angled my new text to match.
