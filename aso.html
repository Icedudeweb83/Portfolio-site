This project involved creating a spooky poster for the Austin Symphony's Halloween children's concert. I began with a few quick sketch ideas! Right-click any of the following images to open them in a new tab and zoom in.

After learning quite a bit of text was going to be added, I decided discretion was the better part of valor. Looking closer at the sketches gave me an idea for some fun subtlety too - the kind that lets you recognize something on a poster up close that you wouldn't see from further away? I also realized that, since Halloween is a two-color holiday, working with a pantone color and limiting myself to its tints would make for a nicely stark image.

So I located some common photo images: human arm bones and a violin.

I removed the hand and wrist bones, and created an image trace of the remaining bones to create a straight "bow." I then traced the violin. The challenge there was matching the pantone tints to the original shadings and colorations on the instrument. But after some careful adjusting I got a satisfactory result.

I then put both objects together, which required some adjusting in sizing and in considering how to intersect the bow at the right place. I realized the best option was to match the bow's tinting to the violin's, and angle it across at just the right place.

I also needed to recreate the word "Maestro" from earlier material. This was simply handtraced.

I then added the copy for the poster. As the concert was for children, and because my image had a more stoic quality, I opted for a fun typeface and styling - of course, keeping everything within the same pantone range. Here is the final image (click to enlarge).
